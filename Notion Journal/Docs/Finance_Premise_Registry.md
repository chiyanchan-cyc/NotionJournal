# Finance Premise Registry

This document defines the user's core macro premises for the Finance system.

The purpose of this registry is not stock picking.
It is to monitor the pulse of the economy, test macro hypotheses, and gather evidence that either corroborates or refutes those views.

## Scope

Primary markets:
- US
- Japan
- Hong Kong
- China

Excluded focus:
- Crypto is not an active research market

## Operating Principle

The system should treat each premise as a testable hypothesis.

For every premise, the system should collect:
- supporting evidence
- refuting evidence
- uncertainty
- potential invalidation signals

## Premises

### P1. Late-Cycle Everything Bubble

Statement:
Credit expansion has created broad asset bubbles, and company-level earnings are being watched primarily as a reflection of overall economic fragility and the timing of a broader crash.

Why it matters:
- Individual companies are viewed as indicators of system stress, not investment targets
- Earnings weakness, guidance cuts, and valuation compression are treated as symptoms of a late-cycle break

Primary markets:
- US
- Japan
- Hong Kong
- China

Signal themes:
- credit exhaustion
- earnings fragility
- valuation compression
- liquidity withdrawal

Key observation targets:
- major company earnings
- central bank meetings
- inflation and labor data
- credit and liquidity stress signals

### P2. AI Capex Burden Without Real Revenue

Statement:
AI is transformative, but durable AI revenue is not yet visible. Hyperscalers and infrastructure providers may be carrying a financing burden that becomes destabilizing before monetization arrives.

Why it matters:
- AI spending may be preserving growth expectations without proving cash returns
- Large enterprises may internalize AI deployment and keep the real cost savings for themselves instead of enriching hyperscaler platforms
- AI infrastructure could become a drag on margins, balance sheets, and future financing

Primary markets:
- US
- Japan

Signal themes:
- hyperscaler capex strain
- AI infrastructure overspend
- weak AI monetization
- enterprise insourcing of AI workloads

Key observation targets:
- hyperscaler earnings and capex guidance
- semiconductor supply chain earnings
- enterprise software commentary
- financing and margin trends

### P3. Consumer Crack and Multiple Compression

Statement:
The consumer will weaken materially, spending will roll over, and earnings multiples will compress, driving a broader market correction.

Why it matters:
- Consumer demand is a transmission mechanism from tighter conditions into earnings stress
- A spending slowdown can damage both cyclical and high-multiple sectors

Primary markets:
- US
- China
- Hong Kong
- Japan

Signal themes:
- discretionary slowdown
- retail weakness
- margin pressure
- guidance cuts
- multiple compression

Key observation targets:
- consumer-facing earnings
- retail and discretionary sector results
- labor market data
- household spending commentary

### P4. Private Credit, Private Equity, CRE, and Data Infrastructure Stress

Statement:
Private credit and private equity have overinvested in aging assets, especially commercial real estate and data infrastructure. When those assets reprice, the financial system will face significant stress.

Why it matters:
- Refinancing pressure and collateral weakness can expose hidden fragility outside public markets
- Stress in private assets can spill into banks, funding markets, and listed equities

Primary markets:
- US
- Hong Kong
- China

Signal themes:
- refinancing stress
- CRE weakness
- data center / warehouse overbuild
- financial spillover

Key observation targets:
- financial institution commentary
- CRE-related stress indicators
- funding market dislocations
- infrastructure and data-center capex commentary

### P5. Gold as Safe Haven, but Only After Forced Liquidation

Statement:
Gold is a long-term safe haven, but it may have front-run the crisis. If private credit stress triggers forced liquidation, even strong assets may crash first, and that liquidation event is the preferred signal to accumulate gold and other high-quality assets.

Why it matters:
- High-quality assets can be sold first during margin calls because they still have liquidity
- The true buying opportunity may come after broad liquidation, not before

Primary markets:
- US
- Japan
- Hong Kong
- China

Signal themes:
- safe-haven overextension
- forced liquidation
- margin-call selling
- high-quality asset drawdown

Key observation targets:
- gold and precious metals behavior
- broad liquidation signals
- funding stress
- cross-asset correlation spikes

### P6. Japan as a Trigger Through Carry Unwind or Energy Stress

Statement:
Japan may become a trigger for global correction. A Japanese energy shock, policy shift, or unwind of the JPY carry trade could propagate into US and global risk markets.

Why it matters:
- Japan is a potential transmission channel into US equities and global liquidity conditions
- Carry unwind and policy normalization can tighten global financial conditions abruptly

Primary markets:
- Japan
- US

Signal themes:
- BOJ normalization
- JPY carry unwind
- energy vulnerability
- cross-border risk transmission

Key observation targets:
- BOJ meetings and guidance
- JPY and rates
- Japanese financial conditions
- spillover into US equities and credit

### P7. Crypto Excluded

Statement:
Crypto is not considered a serious monitoring target for this system.

Why it matters:
- It should not absorb crawler capacity or appear in active watchlists

Primary markets:
- none

Signal themes:
- none

Key observation targets:
- none

### P8. World News Research to Corroborate or Refute the Thesis

Statement:
The system must read world news continuously and map it back to the premises, either to support or to challenge them.

Why it matters:
- The system is only useful if it can collect contradictory evidence, not just confirm pre-existing beliefs
- News should be attached to a premise or theme so it contributes to a coherent macro model

Primary markets:
- US
- Japan
- Hong Kong
- China

Signal themes:
- corroboration
- refutation
- invalidation
- regime-shift monitoring

Key observation targets:
- official releases
- company filings and earnings material
- policy statements
- approved macro journalism

## System Rules

1. The crawler should not roam arbitrarily. It should work from approved sources and map findings back to premises and themes.
2. Company earnings are evidence for macro views, not standalone investment ideas.
3. The system must record both confirming and disconfirming evidence.
4. Any LLM-generated summary must link back to the exact source items used.
5. Calendar events should cover both macro releases and company earnings, but their interpretation should always reference the premise registry.
